1433 Composition Overview

The 1433 composition is a powerful infantry-focused formation used for rallies against specific enemy compositions. This guide will break down the troop counts, gear requirements, hero selection, and battle strategies for optimizing your 1433 rallies.

Key Advantage: The 1433 composition is mainly effective against enemy formations with low cavalry counts (under 1 Million cavalry troops).

When to Use the 1433 Composition

The 1433 composition excels in specific battle scenarios and against certain enemy formations:

  • Effective against enemy formations with low cavalry counts (less than 1 Million)
  • Particularly strong against 442 and 974 compositions
  • Works regardless of enemy gear or formation when their cavalry count is below the threshold
Important: Always scout your opponent before launching a 1433 rally. If they have more than 1 Million cavalry troops, consider a different composition.

Hero & Familiar Selection

The 1433 composition uses full infantry heroes and familiars to maximize the power of your infantry troops.

Hero Selection Tip: If you don't have a specific infantry hero, make sure to replace it with another infantry hero of similar quality. Do not include ranged or cavalry heroes in this composition setup.

Gear Recommendations

For the 1433 composition, prioritize gear that boosts Infantry Attack, Infantry HP, and overall Army Attack stats.

Optimal Gear Stats to Focus:

  • Infantry Attack: Primary stat to maximize
  • Infantry HP: Secondary stat to focus on
  • Army Attack: Tertiary but important overall stat
  • Army HP: Helps reduce losses

Battle Strategy

When using the 1433 composition for rallies, follow these strategic steps:

  1. Scout enemy troops to confirm they have less than 1 million cavalry
  2. Verify the enemy is not in cavalry phalanx (which would counter your infantry-heavy rally)
  3. Set your formation to Infantry Phalanx C
  4. Equip full infantry-focused gear, heroes, and familiars
  5. Fill the rally with the correct 1433 troop distribution
  6. Activate any relevant boosts or talents before launching
  7. Launch the rally and watch for victory!
Strategic Tip: The 1433 composition works best when the enemy doesn't expect it. Try to hide your rally composition until the last moment to prevent them from changing their formation or reinforcing with cavalry.

📜 BASIC KNOWLEDGE ABOUT COMPS, POWER, AND FORMATIONS

1. 💪 What is a "Comp" (Composition)?

Comp = Troop distribution: Infantry / Range / Cavalry.

Always scaled to 20 points.

Examples:

  • 659 = 6 Inf / 5 Range / 9 Cav
  • 866 = 8 Inf / 6 Range / 6 Cav
  • 4412 = 4 Inf / 4 Range / 12 Cav

Rule:

  • Always add parts to 20.
  • If not → double or normalize.

2. ⚡️ What is a "Formation"?

Formation = Which type of troops are deployed FIRST in the fight.

Formation Type Description Best Hero Setup
Infantry Phalanx Infantry front 221 (2 Infantry, 2 Range, 1 Cavalry)
Range Phalanx Range front 122 (1 Infantry, 2 Range, 2 Cavalry)
Cavalry Phalanx Cavalry front 113 (1 Infantry, 1 Range, 3 Cavalry)
Infantry Wedge Wide Inf spread 221 (2 Infantry, 2 Range, 1 Cavalry)
Range Wedge Wide Range spread 122 (1 Infantry, 2 Range, 2 Cavalry)
Cavalry Wedge Wide Cav spread 113 (1 Infantry, 1 Range, 3 Cavalry)

Hero and Familiar Basics:

  • Range Phalanx Rule: Always use exactly 2 Range heroes
  • Infantry Phalanx: 2 Infantry heroes maximize effectiveness
  • Cavalry Phalanx: 3 Cavalry heroes (if available) are optimal
  • Universal Familiar: Jade Wyrm works with all formations
  • Formation-Specific Familiars: Blackwing (Range), Gargantua (Cavalry), Frostwing (Infantry)

3. 🔪 How to Instantly Read a Comp

  • First number = Infantry parts
  • Second number = Range parts
  • Third number = Cavalry parts

Examples:

  • 659 → 6 Inf, 5 Range, 9 Cav
  • 866 → 8 Inf, 6 Range, 6 Cav
Note: If comp looks weird (ex: 4-3-5)? Double it to normalize (becomes 8-6-10).

3. 🔍 Retargeting Mechanics

Retargeting in battle is a critical mechanic that decides who wins in mirror matchups.

Important: In many 4-2-4 vs 4-2-4 Range Phalanx battles, whoever gets their Range killed FIRST often gains battle dynamics in their favor.

When Range squads are eliminated:

  • The timing of Range destruction heavily influences battle outcome
  • Killing enemy Range faster can sometimes be a disservice
  • If both Range are killed simultaneously, expect a close match

Hero Impact on Retargeting:

Heroes play a significant role in determining retargeting sequences - this is why the 2 Range Hero rule is so critical.

  • Hero Tactical Skills: When activated, have slightly longer reach than ordinary squad attacks
  • Squad Positioning: Squads with heroes stay slightly further back compared to unattended squads
  • Positioning Impact: With 1 Range hero, your middle Range squad sticks out toward the enemy
  • Retargeting Result: Enemy Infantry squads ignore the Range squad and move backward, creating a major disadvantage

5. ⚔️ Attack vs HP Stats

Both Attack and HP stats are equally important:

  • HP reduces your losses
  • Attack increases damage to opponents
  • Their synergy creates robust outcomes

Strategic Considerations:

  • Multiple Rallies/Reduced Losses: HP might be more suitable
  • Earning Points (KvK, etc.): Attack stats might be better
  • Increasing Win Chance: Both Attack and HP are ideal

Counter-Intuitive Stat Effects:

  • Increasing HP can sometimes cause more damage to the opponent
  • Increasing Attack can sometimes worsen results in specific matchups (like 442 vs 659 Range Phalanx)
  • Army Attack can sometimes reduce losses more effectively than equivalent HP stats
  • Stats influence familiar activation timing, which can dramatically shift battle results

⚔️ FORMATION GUIDE & MATCHUPS

1. 🔄 Formation Basics

A formation in Lords Mobile determines the order in which troop types receive damage and influences the initial positions of squads on the battlefield.

Common Misconception: The drill in the formation page is misleading due to drastic alterations in troop composition when different formations are selected.

Formation Mechanics:

  • A squad is the smallest unit of troops that physically engages in battle
  • Each icon on the formation page represents one squad
  • Formation determines damage reception order, not direct strength amplification
  • Only the frontline troop type initially receives damage, while all types participate in attacking

3. 🎭 Formation Logic

Range Phalanx vs. Cavalry Phalanx:

Range troops attack enemy cavalry multiple times before the enemy infantry reaches the front line, decimating significant cavalry numbers early.

Infantry Phalanx vs. Cavalry Phalanx:

Enemy cavalry can only slip one attack with max strength before getting touched by your ranged units.

Range Phalanx vs. Infantry Phalanx:

Your cavalry reaches enemy infantry before they can engage your ranged units. Enemy infantry never attacks ranged with maximum strength.

Hero Impact on Formations:

  • Range Phalanx: Always use exactly 2 Range heroes - hero tactical skills affect squad positioning

6. 🔄 Ranged Phalanx Counters

In mixed versus mixed compositions, only the frontline troop type initially receives damage, while all troop types participate in attacking.

Infantry Phalanx vs Ranged Phalanx:

When infantry phalanx confronts ranged phalanx:

  • The infantry inflicts initial damage to the enemy range
  • The opponent's cavalry backline inflicts significant damage to the infantry over time
  • As the infantry count diminishes, so does the attack power against the enemy range

Simplifications for Decision Making:

  • If you see Infantry Heavy → Go Cavalry Phalanx, smash fast
  • If you see Cavalry Heavy → Go Range Phalanx, kite them
  • If you see Range Heavy → Go Infantry Phalanx (or Range Wedge if you want Cav protection)
  • If you see Balanced → Check closely, adjust based on slight bias
  • Mirror (Range vs Range) → Bigger Range count + stronger Infantry wins

Range Wedge Mechanics:

  • Makes Cavalry stay back longer, joining battle after enemy Range is softened
  • Particularly effective against 7112 compositions by disrupting their timing mechanism
  • Requires exactly 2 Range heroes for optimal squad positioning

Infantry Wedge Mechanics:

  • Creates wider Infantry spread, stalling enemy Cavalry charges
  • Gives your Range squads more time to deal damage
  • Excellent defensive formation against Range Phalanx attacks
  • Works best with Infantry heroes that have DEF boosts (Twilight Priestess, Oath Keeper)
  • Frostwing familiar enhances effectiveness with +20% Infantry DEF

Cavalry Wedge Mechanics:

  • Spreads Cavalry wider, creating multiple attack vectors
  • Less commonly used but can be effective in specific scenarios
  • Works best with 3+ Cavalry heroes and Gargantua familiar
  • Most effective against balanced compositions

2. 🎮 Heroes' Role in Retargeting

Heroes play a significant role in determining the retargeting sequence in battles.

Retargeting Explained:

Retargeting in Lords Mobile refers to the change in the target squad receiving damage. Retargeting occurs only when the current squad being attacked is completely killed.

For example, if an INF squad attacks an opposing ranged squad, it can only transition to attacking another enemy squad once the ranged squad is completely killed.

The Issue with One Range Hero:

  • With one range hero, the ranged squad in the middle (unattended by a hero) sticks out towards the enemy
  • The opposing ranged squad (attended by the ranged hero) sticks out away from the opponent
  • Enemy INF squads ignore the range squad in the middle and move backwards
  • This mechanism creates an inherent disadvantage

Examples of Battle Outcomes:

Scenario Result
4-4-2 with two ranged heroes vs. 749 ranged phalanxes Easy win for the 4-4-2
4-4-2 with one ranged hero Loss
4-4-2 with zero ranged heroes Terrible loss
9-5-6 with two ranged heroes vs. 4-4-2 ranged phalanxes Easy win for the 9-5-6
9-5-6 with one ranged hero vs. 4-4-2 ranged phalanxes Nearly impossible to win unless statistically favored
Three ranged heroes in ranged phalanxes Induces a harmful mechanism similar to having one ranged hero

Theoretical Explanation: Hero Tactical Skills

  • The difference in squad position is caused by the hero's tactical skill
  • When activated, it has a slightly longer reach than an ordinary squad attack
  • This keeps the squad position a little bit further away compared to a squad unattended by any hero
  • The activation is characterized by the enlargement of the hero figure on the field
  • When the hero figure shrinks, that's the finish point of the longer reach, followed by a position correction
  • When squads attended by heroes face each other, both activate at similar timings, maintaining relative position
  • If only one side has a hero, the squad without the hero makes an extra step toward the opposing squad
  • This applies to any troop type, not just ranged heroes
