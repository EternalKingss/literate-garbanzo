1. üîÑ Formation Basics

A formation in Lords Mobile determines the order in which troop types receive damage and influences the initial positions of squads on the battlefield.

Common Misconception: The drill in the formation page is misleading due to drastic alterations in troop composition when different formations are selected.

Formation Mechanics:

  • A squad is the smallest unit of troops that physically engages in battle
  • Each icon on the formation page represents one squad
  • Formation determines damage reception order, not direct strength amplification
  • Only the frontline troop type initially receives damage, while all types participate in attacking

3. üé≠ Formation Logic

Range Phalanx vs. Cavalry Phalanx:

Range troops attack enemy cavalry multiple times before the enemy infantry reaches the front line, decimating significant cavalry numbers early.

Infantry Phalanx vs. Cavalry Phalanx:

Enemy cavalry can only slip one attack with max strength before getting touched by your ranged units.

Range Phalanx vs. Infantry Phalanx:

Your cavalry reaches enemy infantry before they can engage your ranged units. Enemy infantry never attacks ranged with maximum strength.

Hero Impact on Formations:

  • Range Phalanx: Always use exactly 2 Range heroes - hero tactical skills affect squad positioning
  • Infantry Phalanx: Infantry heroes with DEF bonuses like Twilight Priestess and Oath Keeper maximize effectiveness
  • Cavalry Phalanx: Barbarian and Storm Fox significantly boost Cavalry performance
  • Wedge Formations: Hero placement affects squad spread - proper hero distribution maximizes wedge effectiveness

Familiar Impact on Formations:

  • Early Activation Familiars: Jade Wyrm, Blackwing, Gargantua, Bon Appeti (4% troop deaths)
  • Late Activation Familiars: Queen Bee, Frostwing (8% troop deaths)
  • Key Formation Pairings:
    • Range Phalanx: Blackwing and Queen Bee maximize effectiveness
    • Cavalry Phalanx: Gargantua is essential
    • Infantry Phalanx: Frostwing provides crucial Infantry DEF
    • All Formations: Jade Wyrm provides universal counter boosts

6. üîÑ Ranged Phalanx Counters

In mixed versus mixed compositions, only the frontline troop type initially receives damage, while all troop types participate in attacking.

Infantry Phalanx vs Ranged Phalanx:

When infantry phalanx confronts ranged phalanx:

  • The infantry inflicts initial damage to the enemy range
  • The opponent's cavalry backline inflicts significant damage to the infantry over time
  • As the infantry count diminishes, so does the attack power against the enemy range

Simplifications for Decision Making:

  • If you see Infantry Heavy ‚Üí Go Cavalry Phalanx, smash fast
  • If you see Cavalry Heavy ‚Üí Go Range Phalanx, kite them
  • If you see Range Heavy ‚Üí Go Infantry Phalanx (or Range Wedge if you want Cav protection)
  • If you see Balanced ‚Üí Check closely, adjust based on slight bias
  • Mirror (Range vs Range) ‚Üí Bigger Range count + stronger Infantry wins

8. üõ°Ô∏è Wedge Formation Advanced Tactics

Range Wedge Mechanics:

  • Spreads Range squads wider, making it harder for enemy Infantry to focus fire
  • Makes Cavalry stay back longer, joining battle after enemy Range is softened
  • Particularly effective against 7112 compositions by disrupting their timing mechanism
  • Requires exactly 2 Range heroes for optimal squad positioning

Infantry Wedge Mechanics:

  • Creates wider Infantry spread, stalling enemy Cavalry charges
  • Gives your Range squads more time to deal damage
  • Excellent defensive formation against Range Phalanx attacks
  • Works best with Infantry heroes that have DEF boosts (Twilight Priestess, Oath Keeper)
  • Frostwing familiar enhances effectiveness with +20% Infantry DEF

Cavalry Wedge Mechanics:

  • Spreads Cavalry wider, creating multiple attack vectors
  • Less commonly used but can be effective in specific scenarios
  • Works best with 3+ Cavalry heroes and Gargantua familiar
  • Most effective against balanced compositions
